Variance Legend

I-P Variance (Individual-Player)

Measures a player's ceiling relative to their projection on any given night. A High I-P Variance player has the power, speed, or chaos factor to completely exceed their projected score — think a stolen base, a two-run shot, a bases-clearing double when nobody expected it. They can also go cold. These are boom-or-bust players: capable of putting up a zero or a game-breaking score. Low I-P Variance players are consistent and reliable — what you see is what you get.

Stack Variance (Team Stack)

Standard DFS formats allow up to 5 hitters from the same team in a lineup. Stack Variance rates how explosive a full 5-man stack from that team can be on any given night. A High Stack Variance team can outperform dramatically — through sequential hitting, power through the lineup, speed on the bases, a soft starting pitcher, or a vulnerable bullpen. Neutral stacks are situational. Low stacks are safer but have a lower ceiling.
